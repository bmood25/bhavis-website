Philosophy & Interests

I believe that innovation is strongest when it’s rooted in a core philosophy. For me, that philosophy is Advaita Vedanta, the nondual school of Hindu thought systematized by Adi Shankaracharya in the 8th century CE. Its central claim is simple to state, but hard to fully grasp: the sense that you are a separate self, cut off from everything else, is not the deepest truth. Beneath that sense of separateness is one undivided consciousness (what Advaita calls Brahman) and everything, including you, is an expression of it.

In more practical terms, it holds that each of us carries an immense, often unrealized strength and courage within. I believe that humane strength should sit at the center of any product. The product itself is simply a means through which we come to realize ourselves.

“Truth, and nothing but truth, is the watchword of the Advaitist.” — Swami Vivekananda
