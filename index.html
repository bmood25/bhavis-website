Philosophy & Interests

I believe that innovation is strongest when it’s rooted in a core philosophy. For me, that philosophy is Advaita Vedanta, the nondual school of Hindu thought systematized by Adi Shankaracharya in the 8th century CE. Its central claim is simple to state and hard to fully grasp: the sense that you are a separate self, cut off from everything else, is not the deepest truth. Beneath that sense of separateness is one undivided consciousness, what Advaita calls Brahman, and everything, including you, is an expression of it.

In more practical terms, it holds that each of us carries an immense, often unrealized strength and courage within. I believe that humane strength should sit at the center of any product. The product itself is simply a means through which we come to realize ourselves.

“Truth, and nothing but truth, is the watchword of the Advaitist.” Swami Vivekananda

Sakshi

“Write what is true for you right now.”

This is my take on AI functioning as a mirror. Sakshi is a journaling app that surfaces structural patterns and tendencies in your journals, voice memos, academic work, and even case studies. It is designed to help you see yourself more clearly, not to tell you what to think or feel. Sakshi is also inspired by the Advaita Vedanta idea that the world is a reflection of the self. Essentially, the AI’s analysis is intended to be a mirror that surfaces your own consciousness back to you, helping you to see yourself more clearly.

Vichara

I needed this, so I built it.

A way for you to store those hard-hitting lines and understand why you resonated with them. Vichara is a reflective reading journal for scripture, poetry, or any text that stops you mid-page. I made this as an alternative to the collage of sticky notes I had on my wall. What’s more, I wanted to learn from this massive cluster by finding themes in my choices. Lo and behold, I use Vichara everyday. It’s built to work across any tradition, or none, whether it’s scripture, a line of poetry, or a quote that caught you off guard: the app doesn’t assume what kind of text matters to you.

Stencil

Ensure that your organization stays true to what it stands for.

An organization-wide alignment tool that compares what a company declares it values against what its people actually experience. Leaders define the org’s stated values and tie ongoing projects to them; employees privately reflect on real moments (decisions, tensions, wins) without ever exposing who said what. Once enough independent voices agree, a pattern surfaces, and Stencil flags where a project’s declared purpose and its lived reality genuinely diverge, or confirms where they align. Built around a structural privacy guarantee, not just a policy promise: no raw reflection is ever readable by anyone but its author, enforced at the database layer.

  • Stack: Next.js, TypeScript, Supabase (Postgres + Row-Level Security)
  • Role: Sole builder / designer
